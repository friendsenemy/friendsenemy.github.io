Situations We Help California Homeowners With

  • Facing Foreclosure: California non-judicial foreclosure runs roughly 4 months. We can close before the trustee sale date.
  • Inherited Property & Prop 19: Since February 2021, Prop 19 limits the parent-child property tax transfer exclusion. If you do not move in within 1 year, the home is typically reassessed at current value, triggering a large tax increase. Many heirs sell rather than absorb the reassessment.
  • Expensive Probate: California statutory probate fees are calculated on gross estate value (4% of first $100K, 3% of next $100K, 2% of next $800K...) for BOTH attorney and executor. Small estates under $184,500 may use simplified procedures.
  • Tired Landlord: California tenant protections (AB 1482 statewide rent caps, local ordinances, Tenant Protection Act) make landlord exits complex. We buy tenanted properties as-is.
  • Wildfire & Insurance Issues: If your property is in a wildfire zone, insurance may be unavailable or unaffordable, killing traditional buyer financing. We buy cash, no insurance contingency.
  • Earthquake & Foundation Damage: Unreinforced masonry, soft-story, or post-quake damaged properties that traditional buyers reject.
  • ADU & Permit Issues: Unpermitted additions, garage conversions, and ADUs that cause appraisal and lender problems.
  • Divorce or Relocation: Cash, firm date, no drama.

How does California Proposition 19 affect selling my inherited house?

Prop 19 (effective February 2021) significantly limits the parent-child property tax transfer exclusion. If you inherit a California home and do not move in within one year as your primary residence, the property will typically be reassessed at current market value — often causing a large property tax increase. Many heirs sell rather than absorb the tax hike. We buy inherited California homes as-is and can close quickly.

California probate is expensive — can I sell quickly?

Yes. California probate is among the most expensive in the country because statutory fees are calculated on the estate's GROSS value, not net (roughly 4% of first $100K, 3% of next $100K, 2% of next $800K, and so on — for BOTH the attorney and the executor). Small estates under $184,500 may qualify for simplified procedures. We buy probate homes as-is to help you close the estate faster.

Are there any fees or commissions?

Zero. No agent commissions, no closing cost fees, no hidden charges. We also cover standard closing costs. The number on your offer is the number you receive at the table.
