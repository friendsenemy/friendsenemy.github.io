Frequently Asked Questions — Selling Your House in Florida

How fast can you close on my house in Florida?

We can close in as few as 7 days anywhere in Florida. Florida is a judicial foreclosure state where the process averages 8-14 months, but we can close before the final judgment. No financing contingencies, no appraisal delays — we pay cash.

Do you buy houses in all Florida cities?

Yes. We buy houses statewide including Miami, Jacksonville, Tampa, Orlando, St. Petersburg, Hialeah, Tallahassee, Fort Lauderdale, Port St. Lucie, Cape Coral, Pembroke Pines, Hollywood, Gainesville, Miramar, Coral Springs, Lehigh Acres, and every rural county in between.

Can you buy a hurricane-damaged Florida house?

Yes. Hurricane damage (wind, storm surge, flood, roof damage) is one of the most common reasons Florida homeowners call us. Traditional buyers often cannot close on damaged properties because insurance binders and lender requirements fall apart. We pay cash, buy as-is, and can close before the next storm season.

How does Florida probate work for inherited houses?

Florida has two primary probate tracks: formal administration (typically 6-12 months) and summary administration (available if the estate is under $75,000 OR the decedent died more than 2 years ago). Florida also allows Disposition Without Administration for very small estates. We buy probate and inherited homes as-is.

What about Florida homestead protection?

Florida's homestead exemption provides strong creditor protection and tax benefits, but it also creates unique transfer rules, including restrictions on devise if the owner is survived by a spouse or minor child. We work with title attorneys to handle these complexities at closing.

Are there any fees or commissions?

Zero. No agent commissions, no closing cost fees, no hidden charges. We also cover standard closing costs. The number on your offer is the number you receive at the table.
