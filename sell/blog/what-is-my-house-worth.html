How Home Values Are Determined

Whether you are planning to sell or just curious, understanding what your home is worth starts with knowing the key factors that drive property values. Unlike online estimators that rely on algorithms, a true home valuation considers the full picture.

Location: Neighborhood, school district, proximity to amenities, and local market conditions all play a major role in your home's value.
Condition: The overall state of the property, including structural integrity, roof age, HVAC systems, and cosmetic appearance, directly impacts value.
Comparable sales: Recent sales of similar homes in your area provide the most reliable benchmark for what buyers are willing to pay.
Square footage and layout: Larger homes and functional floor plans generally command higher prices, though this varies by market.
Market conditions: Whether it is a buyer's or seller's market affects how much you can expect to receive.

Online Estimates vs. Real Offers

Tools like Zillow's Zestimate or Redfin's estimate can give you a rough idea, but they often miss important details. These algorithms cannot see deferred maintenance, recent upgrades, or unique features that affect your home's actual value.
