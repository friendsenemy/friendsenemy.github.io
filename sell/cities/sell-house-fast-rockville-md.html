Maryland Foreclosure Timeline in Montgomery County

Maryland is a judicial foreclosure state, meaning the lender must sue in the Circuit Court before conducting a trustee's sale. For Rockville-area homes, that's the Circuit Court for Montgomery County at 50 Maryland Avenue, Rockville. Here's the sequence:

  1. Default & Loss Mitigation. After ~90+ days delinquent, the lender sends the Notice of Intent (NOI) to Foreclose — a statutory 45-day notice with a Loss Mitigation Application and mediation election form.
  2. Order to Docket. Once the NOI period expires, the lender files the Order to Docket in Montgomery County Circuit Court. This includes the Final Loss Mitigation Affidavit and a certified copy of the deed of trust.
  3. Foreclosure Mediation (optional). The borrower has 25 days after Order to Docket to request mediation through the Office of Administrative Hearings. Mediation typically adds 60-90 days.
  4. Trustee's Sale Scheduling. After 45 days post-Order to Docket (or after mediation closes), the trustee may schedule the auction, advertised in a newspaper of general circulation for three consecutive weeks.
  5. Auction. The sale occurs on the courthouse steps or at an appointed location (some trustees use online platforms). The successful bidder posts a deposit.
  6. Ratification. After a 30-day objection window, the court ratifies the sale. Only at ratification does legal title transfer. Before ratification, the borrower retains the right to sell.
  7. Surplus Funds. If the sale exceeds the debt, surplus goes to the borrower after the auditor's final report — usually months later.

You can sell until ratification — that's the window we work in

Pre-auction and even post-auction (before ratification), we can often close and pay off the secured debt directly, saving your credit from the foreclosure entry. If you're reading an NOI or an Order to Docket right now, every day matters — call us before the auction date.

Maryland Probate & Inherited Rockville Property

If you're the personal representative of an estate that includes Montgomery County real estate, your path through the Montgomery County Register of Wills (50 Maryland Avenue, Rockville) depends on the size and composition of the estate:

Non-Probate Transfers

Property held as tenants by the entireties (spouses) or joint tenants with right of survivorship passes automatically to the survivor. A revocable living trust avoids probate entirely. Maryland also recognizes payable-on-death designations for accounts but not for real estate — there is no Maryland "transfer-on-death deed" statute.

Small Estate

Available when probate assets total ≤ $50,000 (or ≤ $100,000 if the spouse is the sole legatee). Streamlined paperwork, no Orphans' Court hearings, typically 2-3 months to close. Real estate sales are possible under Small Estate authority.

Regular Estate

For probate assets > $50,000 (or > $100,000 with spouse sole legatee). The personal representative posts bond, inventories assets within 90 days, and files an accounting every 6 months. Orphans' Court approval is required for most real estate sales unless the will grants the PR an independent power of sale. Typical timeline: 6-9 months to close the estate, but the real estate can often be sold mid-administration.

Modified Administration

A streamlined Regular Estate option when all residuary legatees consent in writing. Eliminates the accounting requirement and reduces Orphans' Court filings. Good fit for amicable families.

What We Need to Close on an Estate Property

Typically: the personal representative's Letters of Administration (or Letters Testamentary), the will if probated, a death certificate, and a list of all heirs. We coordinate with your estate attorney throughout. Letters of Administration give the PR authority to sign a deed on behalf of the estate — we often close before the estate is fully closed.

MPDU, Transfer Taxes, Ground Rent, Tax Sales & MD Disclosure

Moderately Priced Dwelling Units (MPDU)

Montgomery County's MPDU program (Chapter 25A of the County Code) requires developers of 20+ unit projects to sell or rent a percentage below market. MPDU ownership units carry a 30-year control period with a DHCA-enforced resale price formula, a county right of first refusal, and a recapture provision triggered on the first post-control-period sale. If your MPDU control period has expired, pricing is market-driven but recapture may still apply. We have closed many MPDU transactions and coordinate with DHCA directly.

Maryland & Montgomery County Transfer/Recordation Taxes

On a Rockville sale expect: Maryland state transfer tax 0.5% (0.25% for first-time Maryland homebuyers, paid by seller by default), Montgomery County transfer tax 1.0%, Maryland recordation tax roughly $4.45-$6.75 per $500 depending on price tier. Our cash offers absorb all seller-side transfer/recordation tax, title insurance, and typical closing costs — you net the offer price.

Ground Rent (Rare in MoCo but Not Zero)

Ground rent is overwhelmingly a Baltimore City phenomenon, but a handful of Montgomery County properties — especially older Silver Spring and Takoma Park parcels — carry ground-rent leaseholds. Maryland's 2007 registration requirement extinguished unregistered ground rents (Md. Code Real Prop. § 8-708), but registered ones remain enforceable. We resolve ground-rent payoffs at closing.

Maryland Tax Sale

Montgomery County holds its annual tax sale in June, auctioning tax-lien certificates on parcels with delinquent real property taxes, WSSC assessments, HOA liens that were properly recorded, and certain county liens. The certificate holder must wait 6 months (9 months for owner-occupied homestead) before filing a foreclosure of the right of redemption. If your Rockville property has been sold at tax sale but the certificate holder hasn't yet completed foreclosure, there's still time — we can redeem and close.

Maryland Residential Property Disclosure / Disclaimer

Md. Code Real Prop. § 10-702 requires sellers to deliver either a Residential Property Disclosure Statement or a Disclaimer Statement. Estate sales, trustee sales, and some exempt transfers can use the disclaimer. We routinely accept disclaimer-based as-is sales.

Maryland Lead-Risk Reduction Act

For rental properties built before 1978, the Reduction of Lead Risk in Housing Act requires owners to register with MDE, pay annual fees, and pass lead-risk-reduction inspections at turnover. Non-compliance creates MDE liens and personal liability. We take over the compliance burden on rental purchases.

What is the Maryland foreclosure timeline and can I still sell my Rockville house after an NOI?+

Maryland is a judicial foreclosure state. The lender must send a Notice of Intent (NOI) to Foreclose at least 45 days before filing, offering loss-mitigation and optional foreclosure mediation. The Order to Docket is then filed in the Montgomery County Circuit Court at 50 Maryland Avenue in Rockville, followed by a trustee's sale advertised three weeks in a row, an auction on the courthouse steps or online, and final ratification by the court (typically 30 to 45 days post-sale plus any objection period). You retain full legal title and the right to sell until ratification clears. We can often close before the auction and pay off the secured debt directly.

I inherited a Rockville house — do I have to open probate before I can sell?+

It depends on how title was held. Tenants by the entireties or joint tenants with right of survivorship passes automatically to the survivor, no probate needed. A revocable living trust also bypasses probate. Otherwise you file with the Montgomery County Register of Wills under Small Estate (probate assets ≤ $50,000, or ≤ $100,000 if spouse is sole legatee), Regular Estate (> $50,000, typically 6-9 months), Modified Administration (streamlined when residuary legatees consent), or Summary Administration. We buy estate real estate often before administration closes — the PR's Letters of Administration give authority to sign.

Does the MPDU covenant on my Rockville/MoCo home affect a cash sale?+

Yes. A Moderately Priced Dwelling Unit carries a 30-year control period on ownership units and resale price restrictions enforced by Montgomery County DHCA. The county has a right of first refusal and the resale price is capped by the MPDU formula. If the control period has expired, full market pricing applies, though recapture payments may still apply on the first post-control sale. We handle MPDU transactions regularly and coordinate directly with DHCA.

What are Maryland transfer and recordation taxes in Montgomery County?+

Maryland state transfer tax is 0.5% of sale price (0.25% for first-time Maryland homebuyers). Montgomery County adds a 1.0% county transfer tax. My Rockville rental was built before 1978 — how does the MD Lead Risk Reduction Act affect selling?+
