A direct cash sale removes the parts of the Columbia market you can’t control — the inspection report, the appraisal gap, the financing contingency, the lender balking at an older condo project, the “we’ll credit $15,000 at closing” renegotiation two days before settlement. Maryland & Howard County Rules That Actually Affect Your Sale

We’re not attorneys and nothing here is legal advice. But Columbia sellers lose money and time all the time because they didn’t know the specific rule applied to them. Here is the shortlist we walk through on every call.

The Columbia Association (CA) Annual Charge

Every Columbia property is assessed the Columbia Association annual charge. It is collected with Howard County property taxes, and an unpaid CA charge becomes a lien against the property. When you sell, the title company orders a Columbia Association resale disclosure packet and pays any CA balance at settlement out of the sale proceeds. You do not have to catch up the CA balance before you sell to us — we work through it at the settlement table.

Village Community Association (VCA) Covenants & Assessments

On top of the CA charge, each village has its own Village Community Association — Wilde Lake, Harper’s Choice, Oakland Mills, Long Reach, King’s Contrivance, Owen Brown, River Hill, Hickory Ridge, and Dorsey’s Search each run their own architectural covenants, paint/fence/deck approvals, and assessments. If you have pending covenant issues (a deck without approval, the wrong shade of front-door paint, an unapproved fence), we take those on after closing. You do not have to settle an open violation before we buy.

Maryland Is a Judicial-Foreclosure State

Your lender cannot just auction your Columbia house. For owner-occupied residential property they must send a Notice of Intent to Foreclose at least 45 days before filing, then file an Order to Docket in the Howard County Circuit Court, then serve you Maryland’s mediation notice, and only then schedule a Trustee’s Sale. After the sale the court still has to ratify it before title transfers. From NOI to ratification is often 6–9 months — but if you sell us the property before the sale date, the action is dismissed.

Probate at the Howard County Register of Wills (8360 Court Avenue)

Every Columbia estate goes through the Howard County Register of Wills at 8360 Court Avenue in Ellicott City. Four main paths matter for a sale:

  • Small Estate — gross estate under $50,000 (or under $100,000 when the spouse is sole heir). Fastest path; often resolves in weeks.
  • Regular Estate — anything larger. Longer timeline, Personal Representative manages the sale.
  • Modified Administration — available when all residuary legatees qualify and consent. Streamlined accounting.
  • Summary Administration — limited situations, typically for very small estates owed to the surviving spouse or minor children.

You usually need Letters of Administration and court authority before you can actually close. We coordinate with your probate attorney and the Register directly.

Howard County Transfer & Recordation Taxes

Howard County’s transfer tax is 1.0% of the sale price. Maryland’s state transfer tax is 0.5%. Howard County recordation is $2.50 per $500 of consideration, on top of the state recordation tax. By Maryland custom, these are typically split between buyer and seller — on a cash-buyer sale to us, we absorb normal closing costs so the net figure on your settlement sheet is what you actually keep.

Howard County Annual Tax Sale

Howard County auctions delinquent tax liens each May. After the sale, the purchaser of the certificate must wait the statutory period, then file a complaint in the Circuit Court to foreclose your right of redemption. As long as that judgment has not been entered, you can still redeem the certificate and sell. We close fast and redeem at the table out of the sale proceeds.

Ground Rent (§ 8-708)

Some older Columbia-area parcels — mostly on land that was incorporated into the Rouse plan in the late 1960s — carry a registered ground rent. Under Maryland Real Property § 8-708, the ground-rent holder must be registered with SDAT to enforce it, and any unpaid rent has specific redemption procedures. If your title shows a ground rent, we deal with it at closing — you do not chase an out-of-state ground-rent holder.

§ 10-702 Disclosure or Disclaimer

Maryland’s Residential Property Disclosure/Disclaimer law (Real Property § 10-702) gives you a choice: a full Property Condition Disclosure, or a Disclaimer (sold as-is, no representations). Most of our Columbia cash sales are on the Disclaimer. Federal and state lead-based paint disclosures for homes built before 1978 are a separate, non-optional obligation.

Reduction of Lead Risk in Housing Act

Maryland Environment Article §§ 6-801 et seq. and federal Title X (42 U.S.C. § 4852d) both apply to pre-1978 residential property. That covers original Wilde Lake, Harper’s Choice, Oakland Mills, Long Reach, Owen Brown, and King’s Contrivance homes. A lead-paint disclosure and the EPA pamphlet must be delivered at contract signing, and registered rentals add an MDE layer. We handle all of it at settlement.

Maryland Condominium Act — Resale Certificates

If you are selling a Town Center, Wilde Lake, Owen Brown, or Long Reach condo, the Maryland Condominium Act requires delivery of a resale certificate with specific financial and governance disclosures from the unit-owners association. The management company prepares it; we coordinate the request at the same time we open title so the packet doesn’t bottleneck your closing.
