Maryland & Howard County Rules That Actually Affect Your Sale

We’re not attorneys and nothing here is legal advice. But Ellicott City sellers lose money and time all the time because they didn’t know the specific rule applied to them. Here’s the shortlist we walk through on every call.

Maryland Is a Judicial-Foreclosure State

Your lender can’t just auction your house. For owner-occupied residential property, they must send a Notice of Intent to Foreclose at least 45 days before filing, then file an Order to Docket in the Howard County Circuit Court, then serve you Maryland’s mediation notice, and only then schedule a Trustee’s Sale. After the sale, the court still has to ratify it before title transfers. From NOI to ratification is often 6–9 months — but if you sell us the property before the sale date, the entire action can be dismissed.

Probate at the Howard County Register of Wills (8360 Court Avenue)

Maryland opens estates at the Register of Wills, not the Circuit Court. Four main paths matter for a sale:

  • Small Estate — gross estate under $50,000 (or under $100,000 when the spouse is sole heir). Fastest path; often resolves in weeks.
  • Regular Estate — anything larger, longer timeline, Personal Representative manages the sale.
  • Modified Administration — available when all residuary legatees qualify and consent. Streamlined accounting.
  • Summary Administration — limited situations, typically for very small estates owed to the surviving spouse or minor children.

You usually need Letters of Administration and court authority before you can actually close. We coordinate with your probate attorney and the Register directly.

Howard County Transfer & Recordation Taxes

Howard County’s transfer tax is 1.0% of the sale price. Maryland’s state transfer tax is 0.5%. Howard County recordation is $2.50 per $500 of consideration, on top of the state recordation tax. By Maryland custom, these are typically split between buyer and seller — on a cash-buyer sale to us, we absorb normal closing costs so the net figure on your settlement sheet is what you actually keep.

Howard County Annual Tax Sale

Howard County auctions delinquent tax liens each May. After the sale, the purchaser of the certificate must wait the statutory period, then file a complaint in the Circuit Court to foreclose your right of redemption. As long as that judgment has not been entered, you can still redeem the certificate and sell. We close fast and redeem at the table out of the sale proceeds.

Ground Rent (§ 8-708)

Some older Ellicott City and Howard County parcels carry a registered ground rent. Under Maryland Real Property § 8-708, the ground rent holder must be registered with SDAT to enforce it, and any unpaid rent has specific redemption procedures. If your title shows a ground rent, we deal with it at closing — you don’t chase a 1920s ground-rent holder.

§ 10-702 Disclosure or Disclaimer

Maryland’s Residential Property Disclosure/Disclaimer law (Real Property § 10-702) gives you a choice: a full Property Condition Disclosure, or a Disclaimer (sold as-is, no representations). Most of our Ellicott City cash sales are on the Disclaimer. Federal and state lead-based paint disclosures for homes built before 1978 are a separate, non-optional obligation.

Reduction of Lead Risk in Housing Act

Maryland Environment Article §§ 6-801 et seq. and federal Title X (42 U.S.C. § 4852d) both apply to pre-1978 residential properties. If your Ellicott City home is pre-1978 — which most Historic District, Dunloggin, and Mount Hebron homes are — a lead-paint disclosure and the EPA pamphlet must be delivered at contract signing. Rental registration adds an MDE layer; we’ll handle the paperwork either way.

FEMA Flood Zones & Insurance Disclosure

If your property is in a FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area (AE, AO, A, VE), flood insurance is generally required for any federally-backed mortgage, which is almost every retail buyer. We don’t need a lender, so flood-zone designation alone does not stop us from buying. If you have National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) repetitive-loss history or an open claim, tell us up front — we still buy, we just price accordingly.

The Howard County transfer tax is 1.0% of the sale price, Maryland state transfer tax is 0.5%, and Howard County recordation tax is $2.50 per $500 of consideration (plus the state recordation tax). I inherited a house and probate is open at the Howard County Register of Wills. Can I sell now?+

Often yes. My Ellicott City home went to Howard County tax sale — is it too late to sell?+

Usually not. Howard County holds its tax sale in May each year, and after the sale the certificate holder must wait and then file a complaint to foreclose your right of redemption. Until the Howard County Circuit Court actually enters a judgment foreclosing your redemption rights, you still own the home and can sell it. We have redeemed tax-sale certificates at closing using the sale proceeds. Call us before a foreclosure-of-redemption judgment is entered.

Do I have to file a Maryland § 10-702 Property Condition Disclosure?+

Maryland Real Property Article § 10-702 gives a seller of residential 1–4 unit property the choice between a Disclosure and a Disclaimer. With a cash as-is sale, most of our Ellicott City sellers use the Disclaimer, which confirms the property is sold as-is and that the seller makes no representations. We still give you the form, explain both options, and let you pick. Required federal and state lead-based paint disclosures for homes built before 1978 are delivered separately and are not optional.
