Your Three Main Selling Paths

1. List with a Real Estate Agent

Best for: Sellers who want maximum price, have time (60–90+ days), and can handle showings, negotiations, and potential delays. You'll pay 5–6% in commissions and typically need to prepare the home for market.

2. Sell Directly to a Cash Buyer

Best for: Sellers who value speed, certainty, and simplicity over maximizing every dollar. No commissions, no repairs, close in weeks. Typically yields 70–85% of retail value — but you keep more after fees than many expect.

3. Wait and Watch the Market

Best for: Sellers who have flexibility and believe the market will improve. Carries risk of market change, carrying costs, and opportunity cost. Sometimes the right call — and we'll tell you honestly when we think it is.

Our honest advice: For most sellers who aren't in a distressed situation, listing with a quality agent often makes sense. We'll tell you that directly if it applies to you. We're not here to talk you into a sale that isn't right for you.
