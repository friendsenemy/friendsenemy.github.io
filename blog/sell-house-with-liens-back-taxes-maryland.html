Can I Sell My House With a Tax Lien in Maryland?

Liens and unpaid taxes do not have to stop you from selling. Here is how to move forward.

Home › Blog › Liens & Taxes in MD

Published April 13, 2026 • Pages of Purpose LLC

Yes, you can sell a house with a tax lien in Maryland. Many homeowners think a tax lien, judgment lien, or other encumbrance makes selling impossible — but it does not. We buy properties with liens, back taxes, and title issues all the time. The lien is typically paid off from your sale proceeds at closing, and if there is equity remaining after all liens are satisfied, you keep the difference.

Types of Liens That Affect Maryland Properties

A lien is a legal claim against your property. In Maryland, common types include:

  • Tax liens — Unpaid property taxes, income taxes, or federal tax liens from the IRS.
  • Mechanic's liens — Filed by contractors or subcontractors who were not paid for work done on your property.
  • Judgment liens — Resulting from court judgments, such as unpaid debts or lawsuits.
  • HOA liens — Unpaid homeowner association dues, common in communities across Crofton, Bowie, and Greenbelt.
  • Mortgage liens — Your primary mortgage is technically a lien on the property.

Maryland Tax Sale Process

In Maryland, if you fall behind on property taxes, your county can sell a tax lien certificate at an annual tax sale. The buyer of that certificate earns interest on the unpaid amount, and if you do not pay within the redemption period, they can foreclose on your property. Counties like Prince George's, Charles, and Anne Arundel hold tax sales annually.

Selling your property before the tax sale — or during the redemption period — protects your equity and gives you a fresh start.

Why Traditional Buyers Avoid Properties with Liens

Most traditional buyers and their agents run a title search early in the process. When liens show up, deals fall through. Banks will not issue a mortgage on a property with unresolved title issues, which eliminates the vast majority of buyers from the market.

That is where cash buyers come in. Because we do not rely on bank financing, we can purchase properties with liens and work with title companies and attorneys to resolve them at closing. The lien amounts are typically deducted from the sale proceeds, and you walk away with the remaining equity.

How We Handle Liens at Closing

When you sell to Pages of Purpose LLC, we work with a title company to identify all liens on your property. At closing, the liens are paid off from the proceeds, and any remaining balance goes to you. This process works the same whether your property is in Waldorf, Annapolis, Lexington Park, or anywhere in Maryland.

In many cases, homeowners are surprised to find they still walk away with significant cash even after liens are satisfied. The key is acting before additional penalties, interest, and legal fees pile up.

Do Not Wait — Liens Get Worse Over Time

Every day you wait, interest and penalties continue to accrue. Tax liens in Maryland can accumulate rapidly, and judgment liens can lead to wage garnishment or additional legal action. If you have a property with liens in St. Mary's County, La Plata, or anywhere else in Maryland, the sooner you act, the more equity you preserve.
