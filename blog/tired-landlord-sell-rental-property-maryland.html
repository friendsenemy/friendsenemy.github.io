The Hidden Costs of Being a Landlord

Most landlords underestimate the true cost of owning rental property. Beyond the mortgage, there are property taxes, insurance, maintenance, property management fees, vacancy costs, legal fees for evictions, and the opportunity cost of your time. In counties like Prince George's and Anne Arundel, where property taxes are significant, the math can stop making sense quickly.

Maryland's landlord-tenant laws also add complexity. The eviction process can take months, during which you may receive no rent while still paying all expenses. For landlords with properties in Hyattsville, College Park, or Suitland, tenant turnover in college and government areas adds another layer of unpredictability.

Selling with Tenants in Place

One of the biggest concerns landlords have is whether they can sell with tenants still living in the property. The answer is yes. Pages of Purpose LLC buys rental properties with existing tenants, vacant units, and everything in between. You do not need to go through the stress of evicting tenants before selling — we handle the transition.

This is especially valuable for landlords with properties in Waldorf, Glen Burnie, and Pasadena who may have long-term tenants they do not want to displace or short-term tenants who have been difficult to manage.

No Repairs, No Cleanup, No Staging

Rental properties often show more wear and tear than owner-occupied homes. Years of tenant use can leave a property needing paint, flooring, appliances, and more. Traditional buyers expect these things to be addressed. We do not. We buy rental properties in any condition and handle all the work after closing.

What About Capital Gains?

Selling a rental property can have tax implications, including capital gains tax and depreciation recapture. We always recommend consulting with a tax professional before selling. That said, many landlords find that the relief of being free from the property — combined with the cash in hand — far outweighs the tax considerations.
