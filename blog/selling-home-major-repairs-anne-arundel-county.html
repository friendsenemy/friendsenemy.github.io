If your Anne Arundel County home needs a new roof, foundation repair, mold remediation, or a complete renovation, listing it on the traditional market can feel impossible. Most buyers want move-in ready, and their lenders often will not finance a home with major issues. But your home still has value — and Pages of Purpose LLC will buy it exactly as it is.

Common Repair Issues in Anne Arundel County Homes

Anne Arundel County has a wide range of housing stock, from historic Annapolis homes to 1960s ranchers in Glen Burnie to waterfront properties in Pasadena. Each comes with its own set of common repair challenges:

  • Waterfront and flood damage — Properties in Pasadena, Edgewater, and Annapolis are susceptible to water intrusion, flooding, and moisture-related damage.
  • Aging roofs and siding — Homes in Glen Burnie, Millersville, and Arnold built in the 1960s through 1980s often need full roof and siding replacement.
  • Foundation cracks and settling — Maryland's clay soils can cause foundation issues, especially in older neighborhoods.
  • Outdated electrical and plumbing — Knob-and-tube wiring, galvanized pipes, and other outdated systems are common in older homes and expensive to update.
  • Mold and asbestos — Especially in basements and older construction materials.

Why Traditional Buyers Walk Away

When a buyer's home inspector finds major issues, one of three things happens: the buyer demands a price reduction, the buyer asks you to make the repairs before closing, or the buyer walks away entirely. In competitive markets like Severna Park and Crofton, buyers have plenty of other options — they are not going to take on a project if they do not have to.

This leaves homeowners with damaged properties stuck. They cannot afford the repairs, they cannot find a buyer willing to take on the work, and every month the home sits, it costs more in taxes, insurance, and further deterioration.

How We Value Properties That Need Work

We do not penalize you for your home's condition the way a traditional buyer would. Our offers are based on the property's after-repair value minus our estimated renovation costs. This means you still get credit for your home's location, lot size, and potential — you just do not have to do the work yourself.

We have bought homes throughout Anne Arundel County with every issue imaginable: fire damage in Glen Burnie, storm damage in Pasadena, and decades of deferred maintenance in Odenton. No property is too far gone for us to make a fair offer.
