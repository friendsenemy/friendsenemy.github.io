The True Cost of a Vacant Property

Most people do not realize how expensive a vacant home can be. Beyond the obvious costs like taxes and insurance, there are hidden expenses that add up fast:

  • Increased insurance premiums — Insurance companies charge more for vacant homes because they are at higher risk for damage, theft, and liability claims.
  • Code violations — Counties like Charles County and St. Mary's County enforce property maintenance codes. An unkept lawn or broken window can lead to fines.
  • Vandalism and squatters — Empty homes are targets. Once damage starts, it escalates quickly.
  • Pipe bursts and water damage — A home that is not being heated or maintained is vulnerable to seasonal damage, especially during Maryland winters.
  • Declining value — The longer a home sits empty, the more it deteriorates, and the less it is worth.

Why Southern Maryland Properties Go Vacant

There are many reasons homes in Southern Maryland end up vacant. Some of the most common we see in Waldorf, La Plata, Leonardtown, and Lexington Park include:

  • Inherited properties where the heirs live out of state
  • Former rental properties where the last tenant moved out and the landlord does not want to re-rent
  • Homes that need too much repair for the owner to handle financially
  • Military relocations — common near Lexington Park and NAS Patuxent River
  • Owners who moved but could not sell traditionally due to market conditions or property condition
