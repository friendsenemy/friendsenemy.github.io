How Foreclosure Works in Maryland

Maryland is a judicial foreclosure state, which means your lender must go through the court system to foreclose on your property. The process typically begins when you fall behind on mortgage payments by 90 days or more. Your lender files a lawsuit, and if the court rules in their favor, your home is sold at auction.

The entire process can take several months, but here is what many homeowners do not realize: you can sell your home at any point before the auction. Pre-Foreclosure: Your Window of Opportunity

The period between missing payments and the actual foreclosure auction is called pre-foreclosure. This is your golden window to act. During pre-foreclosure, you still own the home and have the legal right to sell it. Many homeowners in Waldorf, Bowie, Clinton, and Upper Marlboro have used this window to sell fast and move forward with their lives.

The key is acting quickly. The longer you wait, the fewer options you have. A cash buyer can move at the speed you need — no waiting for bank approvals, no repairs, no showings.

What If I Owe More Than My Home Is Worth?

If your mortgage balance exceeds your home's current market value, you may still have options. In some cases, your lender may agree to a short sale, where they accept less than the full balance owed.
