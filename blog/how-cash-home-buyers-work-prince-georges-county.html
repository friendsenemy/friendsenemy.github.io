What Is a Cash Home Buyer?

A cash home buyer is a company or individual that purchases properties directly from homeowners using their own funds — no bank financing, no mortgage approvals, no contingencies. Because there is no lender involved, cash transactions can close much faster than traditional sales, often in 7 to 14 days.

Cash Buyer vs. Traditional Sale in PG County

Here is how the two compare for a typical Prince George's County home:

  • Timeline: Cash sale closes in 7-14 days. Traditional listing takes 3-6 months.
  • Repairs: Cash buyer buys as-is. Traditional buyers expect repairs or credits.
  • Commissions: Zero with a cash buyer. 5-6% with a real estate agent.
  • Closing costs: Cash buyer pays them. Traditional sale splits or seller pays.
  • Certainty: Cash offer is guaranteed. Traditional deals fall through 15-20% of the time due to financing issues.

Red Flags to Watch For

Not all cash buyers are created equal. Here are warning signs:

  • They will not show proof of funds — A legitimate cash buyer can prove they have the money.
  • They pressure you to sign immediately — A fair buyer gives you time to think and consult with your own advisors.
  • They charge fees — You should never pay a cash buyer to buy your home. If someone asks for upfront fees, walk away.
  • They are a wholesaler, not a buyer — Wholesalers put your home under contract and then sell that contract to someone else. Pages of Purpose LLC buys directly.
